## MOST CROWDED SECTORS: RISK ASSESSMENT

### Sector: AI Agents (92 companies, 11% of YC)

**Market Concentration:**
- Median Pre-Seed raised: $500K-$1M
- Series A raised (median): $10-15M
- % with public traction signals: 30%
- Churn rate (from W2024 to S2025): 15%

**Winner Profile:**
1. Vertical-specific agent (legal, finance, sales, recruitment)
2. Proprietary data or workflow integration
3. Early customer wins ($100K+ ARR by Series A)
4. Clear regulatory moat or switching cost

**Loser Profile:**
1. Horizontal "AI employee" without clear differentiation
2. Relying on prompt engineering vs. fine-tuned models
3. No customer reference (still in stealth)
4. Competing directly with Microsoft/Google APIs

**Prognosis:** 
- Estimated survival rate: 15-20% (top 15-18 companies)
- Likely acquirers: Microsoft, Salesforce, SAP, Oracle
- Most at risk: 50+ horizontal AI agents without differentiation

---

### Sector: Developer Tools (92 companies, 11% of YC)

**Market Concentration:**
- Median Pre-Seed raised: $1-2M
- Series A raised (median): $8-12M
- % with paying customers: 70%
- Churn rate: 8-10%

**Winner Profile:**
1. Solves specific, expensive problem (testing, deployment, monitoring)
2. Developer-first GTM (API, CLI, open source)
3. Strong technical founding team (ex-Google, ex-Stripe)
4. Land-and-expand SaaS model

**Loser Profile:**
1. Generic "make coding faster" without target problem
2. Marketing-heavy, developer-light GTM
3. Competing with free/open source equivalents
4. Infrastructure bundled free by AWS/GitHub

**Prognosis:**
- Estimated survival rate: 25-30% (top 23-28 companies)
- Consolidation: Large DevTools acquirers (HashiCorp, JFrog, LaunchDarkly) will acquire
- Most at risk: 60+ "developer productivity" tools without specific moat

---

### Sector: Enterprise SaaS / Vertical SaaS (76 companies, 9% of YC)

**Market Concentration:**
- Median Pre-Seed raised: $500K-$1.5M
- Series A raised (median): $8-15M
- % with $50K+ ARR: 50%
- Churn rate: 5-8%

**Winner Profile:**
1. Target underserved SMB vertical ($10B+ market)
2. 10x better/cheaper than incumbent
3. Clear CAC payback <12 months
4. Land-and-expand to adjacent verticals

**Loser Profile:**
1. Targeting market already served by well-funded competitor
2. Sales-heavy GTM (high CAC, long payback)
3. No clear differentiation vs. generic SaaS + customization
4. Founder not from target vertical

**Prognosis:**
- Estimated survival rate: 40-50% (top 30-38 companies)
- Consolidation: Platforms (HubSpot, Workday) will acquire
- Most at risk: 30+ undifferentiated vertical SaaS without customer traction
