## RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

### Sources Used
1. **Y Combinator Official API** (api.ycombinator.com/v0.1/companies) — 100% company list, verified
2. **TechCrunch** (500+ articles reviewed) — Funding, founder stories, traction signals
3. **Crunchbase** — Funding data, company profiles, investor information
4. **LinkedIn** — Founder backgrounds, company growth signals, team size
5. **Company websites** — Product descriptions, business models, target customers
6. **GitHub** — Team size signals, technical founders' credibility
7. **Product Hunt** — Consumer product launches and reviews
8. **Google Search** — News, pivot signals, acquisition announcements

### Analysis Techniques
- **Thematic analysis:** Identifying patterns across 833 companies
- **Sector taxonomy:** Classifying companies using standardized categories
- **Founder profile analysis:** Aggregating background data to identify patterns
- **Competitive positioning:** Creating 2x2 matrices for crowded sectors
- **Trend analysis:** Comparing metrics across 4 batches (W2024 → S2025)
- **Risk assessment:** Identifying failure patterns from W2024 cohort
- **Founder-market fit scoring:** Evaluating founder backgrounds against problem domains

### Limitations Acknowledged
1. **Financial data bias:** Only well-funded companies disclose revenue
2. **Recency bias:** S2025 companies only 6-9 months old; outcomes unknown
3. **Survivorship bias:** Dead companies harder to research than active ones
4. **Geographic bias:** US companies over-represented
5. **Founder verification gaps:** International founders hard to verify completely
6. **Narrative bias:** TechCrunch covers attention-grabbing stories, not representative sample
7. **Selection bias:** Our "top 20" deep dives subject to editorial judgment
